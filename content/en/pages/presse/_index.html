About Jean-Jacques Birgé

Jean-Jacques Birgé is one of those artists that can't leave you indifferent. An incredible story, a eloquence as rich to narrate it, he's as much a composer, a sound designer, an author, and a man of image.
Citizenjazz

Difficult to classify musically, he belongs to the lineage of encyclopedist composers, such as Charles Ives, İlhan Mimaroğlu, Frank Zappa, René Lussier, François Sarhan, Jonathan Pontier or John Zorn, all atypical and often self-taught.
France Musique

As for Jean-Jacques Birgé, a genius jack-of-all-trades, he participated in the writing of some of the most beautiful multimedia pages, first on CD-Rom with Alphabet, the Théâtre de minuit or even Machiavelli, written with Antoine Schmitt, a title which prefigured some of the best graphic music software.
Libération
