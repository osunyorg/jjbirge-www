Jonathan Rosenbaum was film critical for the Chicago Reader from 1987 to 2008. Born in Alabama in 1943, the son and grandson of movie exhibits, he green up in a house designed by Frank Lloyd Wright. While living in Paris (1969-1974), he worked as an extra for Robert Bresson and as a script consultant for Jacques Tati, before working as assistant editor for Monthly Film Bulletin and staff writer for Sight and Sound in London (1974-1977).
