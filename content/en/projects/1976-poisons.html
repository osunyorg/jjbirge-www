body: >-


24 hours of unissued music! From 1976 to 1977, Un Drame Musical Instantané, featuring Jean-Jacques BIRGÉ, Bernard VITET et Francis GORGÉ , records improvisations which titles are all named from poisons. In 1977, singer TAMIA has joined the trio for one year. All these recordings made by Jean-Jacques Birgé , 7 rue de l'Espérance, Paris (except Strychnine which was recorded live at France Musique for Les astronotes/Non écrites by Pierre Lattès and Joséphine Markovits). The Drame always prefered to call these improvisations "instantaneous compositions" in opposition to "preliminary compositions". Birgé's synthétiseur is an ARP 2600. Gorgé's guitar is a Gibson SG Standard and his bass a Fender Precision. Photo - Thierry Dehesdin Film directed by Emmanuelle K for free TV channel Antène 1 on Blog!
